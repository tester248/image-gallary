The Oceans

Most coral reefs grow in tropical waters. The largest coral reef is the Great Barrier Reef, one of the “Seven Natural Wonders of the World,” which extends more than 1,930 kilometers (1,200 miles) off the eastern coast of Australia..

Jellyfish Sanctuary
One of the bioengineers interested in biogenic ocean mixing is National Geographic Emerging Explorer Kakani Katija. “I would say my original interests were on animal swimming or jellyfish propulsion,” she says. “In that investigation, some of this work . . . suggested that swimming animals can impart a lot of [ocean] mixing.” Jellies thrive in every ocean, including the tropical Pacific of the Philippines, above.

Hol-Chan Marine Reserve, Belize
Hol-Chan Marine Reserve is Belize's oldest marine protected area (MPA). Hol-Chan means "little channel" in the ancient Mayan language. Hol-Chan is a section of coral reef (called a quebrada) about 23 meters (75 feet) wide and nine meters (30 feet) deep.
