Towering Trees
Trees in the emergent layer can grow as tall as 61 meters (200 feet). Foliage is often sparse on tree trunks, but spreads wide as the trees reach the sunny upper layer. Here, moss covers the towering trees of the Great Bear Rainforest in British Columbia, Canada.

Jackson's Chameleon Animals call the understory home for a variety of reasons. Many take advantage of the dimly lit environment for camouflage. This chameleon (Chamaeleo jacksoni), native to East Africa and an invasive species in Hawaiian rainforests, can even change colors to camouflage itself.
